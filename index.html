A little more context

How I got here.

I spent years teaching before I started building software. That matters because I still think like a teacher: start with the real confusion, name it clearly, and build the smallest thing that makes the next step obvious.

I am not coming at this from a traditional engineering biography. I came in through language, explanation, and the stubborn feeling that too many useful tools stayed trapped behind someone else's gate. AI changed that. It turned software from a closed profession I was orbiting into a medium I could work in directly.

Now I build apps, websites, and internal tools for people who can already feel the friction in their work, even if they do not yet know the shape of the fix. That is the through-line: translate a fuzzy problem into something concrete enough to ship.

If you want the short version, it is this: teacher brain, builder tools, very little patience for waste, and a real interest in making software feel more human than the process that usually produces it.
